# What is workflow (processes)

A workflow is a repeatable process made of clearly defined steps, rules, and decisions.

In Hubleto, a workflow pipeline is the visual sequence of those steps. It shows where a record currently is in the process and what stage should come next.

Without a defined workflow, similar tasks are often handled differently by different people. That usually leads to inconsistent results, slower handoffs, and less visibility into what is blocked or already finished.

## Why workflows matter

A good workflow helps your team in several practical ways:

* **Consistency**: similar work follows the same path every time.
* **Efficiency**: once the steps are visible, it is easier to spot delays and remove bottlenecks.
* **Accountability**: everyone can see who is responsible for the current stage of the process.

Example pipeline:

`New lead` -> `Qualified` -> `Proposal` -> `Negotiation` -> `Won`

## How workflows are used in Hubleto

Hubleto uses workflows for multiple record types, including deals, projects, tasks, orders, leads, campaigns, and invoices.

Depending on the app and screen, you may see a workflow in two common forms:

* as a **workflow bar** directly on a record,
* as a **pipeline or board view** that groups records by stage.

Workflow steps can be configured with:

* a name,
* an order,
* a color for visual orientation,
* optional metadata such as tags or probability, depending on the workflow type.

## Can workflows be customized?

Yes. Workflows in Hubleto are editable, so teams can adapt the stages to match their own internal process instead of forcing everyone into one rigid structure.

That is useful when:

* your sales team uses a specific deal pipeline,
* project work needs custom delivery phases,
* task management requires statuses that fit your real approval flow.
